Slow Cooker Texas Smoked Beef Brisket

Description

Beef brisket is a cut of meat that comes from the breast or lower chest of a cow. It is a tough, yet flavorful cut that benefits from long, slow cooking methods to break down the connective tissues and create a tender, succulent texture. This slow-cooked classic has become a popular dish in many restaurants and homes, known for its rich, smoky flavor and melt-in-your-mouth texture.

Ingredients :

  • 4-5 pound beef brisket (point cut preferred)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 teaspoon liquid smoke
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper

Steps :

  1. Trim the brisket: Remove excess fat and any connective tissue.
  2. Season: Rub the brisket generously with garlic powder, onion powder, black pepper, and cayenne pepper.
  3. Slow cooking: Place the brisket in the slow cooker, fat side up. Pour beef broth and Worcestershire sauce over the brisket.
  4. Cook: Cook on low for 10-12 hours, or on high for 6-8 hours. The brisket is done when it's tender and easily shredded.
  5. Rest: Let the brisket rest for at least 30 minutes before slicing or shredding.
